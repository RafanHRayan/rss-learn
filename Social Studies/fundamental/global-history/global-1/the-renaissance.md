# The Renaissance

After the Black Death and Crusades, people begin to doubt the church, slowly starting to think less about religion and more about themselves, their surroundings, and new types of knowledge.

During this era, [the West](#user-content-fn-1)[^1] continues to develop. This includes the Mongol Empires; the east African, West African, South Asian, and Middle Eastern Empires; and empires developing in the Americas. A lot happens during this time of the Renaissance[^2].

## The Italian Peninsula

A result of the Crusades was increased trade with the Middle East and Asia. Most of the trade routes went through Italy, so the Italian peninsula became the gateway for trade for Europeans, if they wanted to do business in the Middle East or Asia.

Despite this, Italy wasn't united but rather a group of independent city-states.

{% hint style="info" icon="circle-question" %}
Because the Italian city-states were independent, the same rule as Greek city-states applies. Italians did not identify as Italian, but rather as Roman, Venetian, Florentine, etc.
{% endhint %}

One such city-state, Florence, became the center of the Renaissance, as most of the new ideas originated there. In general, however, competition between city-states caused them to innovate in hopes to do better than other city-states. There was a lot of economic activity in each city wealth generation rising standard of living.

Humanism is the belief that humans have the capacity to make choices and shape their own lives, rather than being predetermined by fate of divine intervention. It emphasized education and learning from [the classical cultures](#user-content-fn-3)[^3]. For Western Europeans, Italy was a place where people could literally see artifacts from Classical Rome to inspire them.

### The Medicis

The Medicis were one of the most famous political families that existed during the Renaissance. They were influential from about 1350-1750’s. The Medicis influenced the government in Florence, resulting in some of them even becoming popes.

The Medicis were patrons of the arts. They had the money to fund the artwork of artists like Brunelleschi, Michelangelo, Donatello. The art that the Medicis funded made Florence famous.

#### Money Lending

The Medicis simultaneously revolutionized business and banking. They were a family in the right place at the right time with a lot of money. They basically invented capitalism, creating interest. This, however, was frowned upon as the Bible stated that money received without effort is bad.

## Renaissance Art

Medieval learning planted the seeds of curiosity that developed during the Renaissance. After the Crusades, reintroduction to trade markets. Along with this, new access to the Islamic Golden Age introduced new types of knowledge related to art.

Having patrons like the Medicis and other royalties allowed people to make new types of art.

### Comparison of Art

Between the medieval and renaissance periods, the following differences can be seen...

* Less religious themes were present, with more focus on Humanism and education.
* More realistic images using light and shadow. This made paintings have more realistic proportions.
* The use of bright colors and oil paints on canvas.
* Perspective to add depth to paintings.

Because of these differences, medievals paintings felt flat and two-dimensional. Renaissance paintings—on the other hand—used vanishing lines of perspective to focus the viewer's eye. This is one of the most significant innovations of Renaissance art.

Renaissance art also reflected Classical values from Greece and Rome. They showed motion, nudity, and the subject matter always represented the "Classics" in some way.

## Northern Renaissance

Italy wasn't the only region experiencing a Renaissance period, however. Trade routes helped to spread Renaissance ideas from Italy to Northern Europe as well.

Though many ideas were similar, there were also differences...

* They weren't as interested in the Classics. More focus on the common man.
* No nudity was present.
* There was more focus on [interior paintings](#user-content-fn-4)[^4].

Reflections and angles were used a lot in European Renaissance paintings. Mirrors in paintings actually looked like they reflected.

## Printing

Before printing, anything that needed to be copied was done by hand. This was called illuminated manuscript. It was very pretty but took a long time to make. Then, people bagan to use stamps but they took a long time to make, were fragile, and would sometimes only be able to be used for a specific book.

### The Printing Press

Finally, the printing press was invented by Johann Gutenberg. He used Chinese style movable type, but since there are fewer characters in the Latin alphabet than in Chinese, it was much faster.

This substantially lowered price of books so more people could afford them. Books now written in the vernacular so people who had no classical education could now read the classics. This all led to increased literacy, helping to spread Renaissance ideas to Northern Europe.
