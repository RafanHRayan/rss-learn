The Crusades led Europe into getting new technological advancements, including the magnetic compass, astrolabe, and lateen sail.

## The Lateen Sail

Faster ships were invented. The new ships had the lateen sail. This sail was triangular instead of the traditional rectangular sails. These sails allowed Europe to sail into the wind.

## Voyages of Purpose

### Voyages for Religious Spread

People felt loyal to Christianity and wanted to spread the religion. Protestants and Catholics competed to spread their “version” of Christianity. However, Europe was at a severe disadvantage. They weren't located on the middle of the trade routes that connected global trade networks of the time. Specifically, there was no easy access to the southeast, where a lot of trade was occurring.

### Voyages for Spice

At the time, Eastern goods came into the Mediterranean through the Middle East from the Indian Ocean. But, Middle Eastern merchants charged heavy taxes. Once it got to Europe, it was far more expensive than it was originally in the east.

Southeast Asia had spices[^1]. The Europeans wanted them badly.

## The Portuguese

The Indian Ocean Trade network had been controlled by Muslims for centuries. During this time, trade between India, the Middle East and East Africa had been peaceful. After the Dum Diversos, the Portuguese have a motivation to end the Muslim stronghold.

The Portuguese were highly focused on trade, not empire-building. Because of this persistence, they were the first Europeans to sail directly to India by going around Africa. Portugal created a “trading post empire” within the Indian Ocean.

This was easy to do because merchant ships in the Indian Ocean weren’t heavily armed and Portuguese ships had cannons; merchant ships obviously didn't.

The Portuguese then required all merchant vessels to purchase a cartaz (pass) to sail throughout the region. Additionally, they charged merchant vessels taxes about 10% of their cargoes.

### The East India Companies

European countries began to initiate sponsored voyages. The East India Companies allowed many countries to attempt to gain access, but few were able to gain the successes of the Dutch.

The British, Danish, Duth, French, Swedish, and Portuguese East India Companies were all founded from the 1600s to the early 1700s.

The East India Companies were private trading companies that countries created with merchant investors to raise money and share risks. Buying a portion of the company meant your risk would be lower. This was called joint-stock.

The East India Companies were essentially “a state within a state”. These companies were granted charters by their governments that allowed them to make war, govern conquered peoples, and hold trading monopolies.

The Dutch and British were the strongest. They overtook Portugal within the Indian Ocean network in the early 1600s. The Dutch focused on Indonesia and the British focused on India.

[^1]: e.g. cardamoms, cinnamon, black pepper, ginger, cayenne, etc.
