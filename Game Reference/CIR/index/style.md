# Style

**Style** is one of the main [game mechanics](mechanics.md) of Combat Initiation. It's a show of skill and current round contribution, more likely your performance and actions in combat. It is the only way to receive score.

A gauge bar is used to show the current amount of style a player has. By receiving any style, the style box will show up, showing the style gauge, current style bonuses, tier, and color, and aerial multiplier. Style gradually goes down as less damage or style bonuses are gained, this can affect players that use the [Red Stratobloxxer](https://combatinitiation.miraheze.org/wiki/Red_Stratobloxxer).

[Taunting](https://combatinitiation.miraheze.org/wiki/Taunts) will prevent style decrease, which is useful for intermissions and boss spawns, though, if attacked while taunting, the style will still decrease, although at a slower rate, however, this feature can be useful for getting the achievement "Maximum Overdrive", although it is risky.

## Style Bonuses <a href="#style_bonuses" id="style_bonuses"></a>

By doing specific actions, you obtain style bonuses, which increase your style.

### Tiers <a href="#tiers" id="tiers"></a>

There are multiple achievable tiers:

* Dull
* Cool
* Blox
* Awesome!
* Superb!!
* XTREME!!!

Healing and lifesteal is affected by tiers, the higher the tier is, the more healing you will be able to receive. Damage can also be influenced by Red Stratobloxxer based on the tier, starting at -20% damage at dull to +60% damage at xtreme.

After a stage, the game will show what tier you got for each of [the three categories](#user-content-fn-1)[^1] by representing [the first letter of the tier along with color on the boxes on the right](#user-content-fn-2)[^2]. You can also see your and other players style by looking to the left of their username on the leaderboard along with their score.

Tiers increase how much score is given for a style bonus.

* Dull: 0%
* Cool: 25%
* Blox: 50%
* Awesome!: 100%
* Superb!!: 200%
* XTREME!!!: 400%

### Aerial Multiplier <a href="#aerial_multiplier" id="aerial_multiplier"></a>

By staying in the air, an aerial multiplier starts to increase, capping at a multiplier of 2. Upon landing, it will instantly change back to 1.
