# Solids

For this point on, two-dimensional figures have been the main focus, but if we look at another axis, we can find more complex ideas that use applications from two-dimensional figures.

## Defining Solids

A **solid** is a three-dimensional figure.

### Basic Solid Terminology

A **face** of a solid is a flat, two-dimensional surface that forms part of the boundary of a solid object.

An **edge** of a solid is a straight line segment where two faces of a polyhedron meet. An edge can also be curved.

A **vertex** of a solid is a point where three or more edges meet.

A **base** of a solid the face upon which the solid is oriented. A solid can have multiple bases.

A **lateral face** of a solid is a face that is not a base.

A **lateral edge** of a solid is the edge formed by the intersection of two adjacent lateral faces of a polyhedron.

**Volume** is the amount of space that a solid takes up. It is essentially how many 1x1x1 cubes can fit inside the solid.
