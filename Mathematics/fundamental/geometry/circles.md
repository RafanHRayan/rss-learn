# Circles

## Defining a Circle

A circle is commonly known to many, yet it is much more complex.

<details>

<summary><i class="fa-note">:note:</i> Definition of A Circle</summary>

A circle is the set of all points equidistant from a center point.

</details>

<details>

<summary><i class="fa-note">:note:</i> Definition of A Center</summary>

A point is the center of a circle if and only if it is the point in which all the points of a circle are equidistant.

</details>

<details>

<summary><i class="fa-note">:note:</i> Definition of A Radius</summary>

A segment is the radius of a circle if and only if it is the distance in which every point of a circle is from the center.

</details>

## Fundamental Figures

<details>

<summary><i class="fa-note">:note:</i> Definition of A Chord</summary>

A segment is a chord of a circle if and only if it connects two points of a circle.

</details>

<details>

<summary><i class="fa-note">:note:</i> Definition of A Secant</summary>

A line is the secant of a circle if and only if it passes through two points of a circle.

</details>

<details>

<summary><i class="fa-note">:note:</i> Definition of A Diameter</summary>

A segment is a diameter of a circle if and only if it connects two points of a circle and passes through the center.

</details>

<details>

<summary><i class="fa-note">:note:</i> Definition of A Tangent</summary>

A line is tangent to a circle if and only if it meets the circle at exactly one point.

</details>

<details>

<summary><i class="fa-note">:note:</i> Definition of A Point of Tangency</summary>

A point is the point of tangency if and only if it is the point where a circle and a tangent meet.

</details>



<details>

<summary><i class="fa-note">:note:</i> Definition of A Circular Arc</summary>

A circular arc is a part of a circle.

</details>

<details>

<summary><i class="fa-note">:note:</i> Definition of A Semicircle</summary>

A semicircle is the arc intercepted by a diameter.

</details>

## Fundamental Theorems

<details>

<summary><i class="fa-note">:note:</i> Radius Congruence</summary>

All radii of the same circle are congruent.

</details>

<details>

<summary><i class="fa-note">:note:</i> Tangent Perpendicularity</summary>

A tangent to a circle is always perpendicular to the radius that meets its point of tangency.

</details>

## Radians

Radians are another way to reference angle and arc measures, being a popular alternative to degrees. A radian is the ratio of the arc length to the radius. There are $$2\pi{}$$ radians in a circle, meaing that $$180\degree{}=\pi{}\text{ rad}$$.
